One Skillet Mediterranean Chicken

Description

This is a delicious and healthy mediterranean diet meal. The original recipe can be found on themediterraneandish.com.

I chose to add this meal because it is so healthy and refreshing, but at the same time, extremely simple and can be made in a single pan. Try chicken breasts for a healthier lean cut or chicken thighs for a more flavorful fattier cut. Diced tomatoes can be fresh or canned, but I prefer fresh.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts of equal size
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • Olive oil
  • 1/2 cup dry wine (optional)
  • 1 large lemon, juice of
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup finely chopped red onion
  • 1 1/2 cup small-diced tomatoes
  • Parsley
  • Crumbled feta, optional

Steps

  1. Pat the chicken breasts dry. On each side of the chicken breasts make three slits through.
  2. Spread 2 tbsp garlic on both sides; insert some garlic into the slits. Season the chicken breasts on both sides with salt, pepper and ½ tbsp of dried oregano.
  3. In a large cast iron skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil on medium-high. Brown the chicken on both sides.
  4. Add the white wine and let reduce by ½ then add the lemon juice and ½ cup of chicken broth.
  5. Sprinkle the remaining oregano on top. Reduce the heat to medium. Cover with a lid or tightly with foil. Cook for 10-15 mins turning the chicken over once (chicken's internal temperature should reach 165 degrees F.)
  6. Uncover and top with the chopped onions and tomatoes. Cover again and cook for only 3 minutes.
  7. Finally add the parsley and feta cheese. Can be served with a light pasta, rice, or couscous.
